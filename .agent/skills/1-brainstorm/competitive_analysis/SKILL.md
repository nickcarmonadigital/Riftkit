# Competitive Analysis Skill

> **PURPOSE**: Map your competitive landscape so you know exactly who you are up against, where the gaps are, and how to position your product to win.

## 🎯 When to Use

- You are starting a new product and need to understand the market
- You are writing a proposal or pitch and need competitive positioning
- You want to find feature gaps your competitors have not filled
- You are deciding on pricing and need reference points
- A stakeholder asks "how are we different from X?"

---

## Step 1: Identify Your Competitors

Every product has three types of competitors. List at least 2-3 in each category.

| Type | Definition | Example (if you build a project management tool) |
|------|-----------|------------------------------------------------|
| **Direct** | Solves the same problem the same way | Asana, Monday.com, Jira |
| **Indirect** | Solves the same problem a different way | Spreadsheets, email threads, sticky notes |
| **Potential** | Could enter your market easily | Notion (adding PM features), Slack (adding tasks) |

### Competitor Discovery Checklist

- [ ] Search "[your product type] alternatives" on Google
- [ ] Check G2, Capterra, or Product Hunt for category leaders
- [ ] Ask potential customers "what do you use today?"
- [ ] Search Reddit and Twitter for complaints about existing tools
- [ ] Check Crunchbase for funded startups in your space

Compare what each competitor offers. Use checkmarks, X marks, or ratings.

| Feature | Your Product | Competitor A | Competitor B | Competitor C |
|---------|-------------|-------------|-------------|-------------|
| Core feature 1 | Planned | Yes | Yes | No |
| Core feature 2 | Planned | Yes | Partial | Yes |
| Differentiator 1 | Planned | No | No | No |
| Differentiator 2 | Planned | No | No | No |
| Mobile app | Planned | Yes | Yes | No |
| API access | Planned | Yes | No | Yes |
| Free tier | Planned | Yes | No | Yes |

### Example: Task Manager Feature Matrix

| Feature | TaskFlow (You) | Asana | Trello | Todoist |
|---------|---------------|-------|--------|---------|
| Task creation | Yes | Yes | Yes | Yes |
| Subtasks | Yes | Yes | No | Yes |
| AI auto-prioritization | Yes | No | No | No |

**Insight**: Your AI auto-prioritization and unlimited free tier are differentiators nobody else has.

## Step 3: Positioning Map

A positioning map plots competitors on two axes that matter most to customers. It reveals open spaces in the market.

### How to Build One

1. Pick two factors your customers care about most (e.g., price and ease of use)
2. Place each competitor on the 2x2 grid
3. Look for the empty quadrant -- that is your opportunity

```
                    Easy to Use
                        |
           Trello       |     Todoist
        (free, simple)  |  (affordable, clean)
                        |
  Cheap ————————————————+———————————————— Expensive
                        |
           Notion       |     Asana
        (complex, free) | (complex, pricey)
                        |
                   Hard to Use
```

**Your opportunity**: If no one occupies "Easy + Affordable with AI," that is your positioning.

### Common Axis Pairs

| Axis 1 | Axis 2 | Good For |
|--------|--------|----------|
| Price | Features | Finding the value sweet spot |
| Ease of use | Power/depth | Finding the simplicity gap |
| Individual | Team-focused | Finding underserved user segments |
| Generic | Industry-specific | Finding niche opportunities |

### Example SWOT: Analyzing Trello

```
Competitor: Trello
Website: trello.com
Founded: 2011 (acquired by Atlassian)
Funding: Backed by Atlassian ($50B+ company)

STRENGTHS
- Extremely easy to learn
- Strong brand recognition
- Generous free tier
- Huge plugin ecosystem

WEAKNESSES
- No built-in reporting or analytics
- Limited views (mostly just Kanban)
- Power features require expensive upgrade
- No AI capabilities

- Users outgrow Trello and need more power
- AI features that Trello does not offer
- Better reporting without the complexity of Jira

THREATS
- Atlassian could add AI to Trello at any time
- They have massive distribution advantage
- Price wars -- they can afford to stay free
```

## Step 5: Differentiator Identification

Your differentiator is the reason a customer picks you over alternatives.

### Differentiator Categories

| Type | Description | Example |
|------|-----------|---------|
| **Feature** | You have something they do not | "Only tool with AI prioritization" |
| **Price** | You are cheaper or offer more value | "Enterprise features at startup prices" |
| **Audience** | You serve a specific group better | "Built specifically for freelancers" |
| **Experience** | You are significantly easier or faster | "Set up in 2 minutes, not 2 hours" |
| **Integration** | You connect to their existing tools | "Works inside Slack, no tab switching" |
| **Philosophy** | You have a different approach | "Privacy-first -- your data never leaves your server" |

### Finding Your Differentiator

Answer these three questions:

1. **What do customers complain about** with existing tools? (Check review sites, Reddit, Twitter)
2. **What do you do** that nobody else does?
3. **What can you be the best at** given your team and resources?

Your differentiator sits at the intersection of all three.

## Step 6: Pricing Comparison

| Competitor | Free Tier | Starter | Pro | Enterprise |
|-----------|-----------|---------|-----|-----------|
| Competitor A | Yes (limited) | $10/mo | $25/mo | Custom |
| Competitor B | No | $15/mo | $30/mo | $50/mo |
| Competitor C | Yes (generous) | $8/mo | $20/mo | Custom |
| **Your Product** | ? | ? | ? | ? |

### Pricing Strategy Options

| Strategy | When to Use | Example |
|----------|------------|---------|
| **Undercut** | You want fast adoption | Price 20-30% below market leader |
| **Match** | Your differentiator is features, not price | Same price, more value |
| **Premium** | You serve a niche with high willingness to pay | 2x price, 10x support and customization |
| **Freemium** | You need a large user base first | Free tier with paid upgrade |

## Step 7: Market Gap Summary

After completing the analysis, summarize your findings in one table:

| Gap Found | Evidence | Your Opportunity |
|-----------|---------|-----------------|
| No AI features in any competitor | Feature matrix shows zero AI | Build AI-first experience |
| Freelancers underserved | All competitors target teams | Position for solo users |
| Complex onboarding everywhere | Reviews mention steep learning curves | Build 2-minute setup |
