# Client Discovery Skill

> **PURPOSE**: Quickly understand what a client needs in 15-30 minutes.

## 🎯 When to Use

- First call with a potential client
- Starting a new project
- Qualifying a lead before sending a proposal

---

## 🎯 Discovery Goals

By the end of discovery, you should know:

1. **What they do** (business type, services, customers)
2. **What they have** (current state, tools, pain points)
3. **What they need** (goals, timeline, budget)
4. **If they're a fit** (budget, expectations, communication)

## 📞 Call Structure (15-30 min)

### Opening (2 min)

```
"Thanks for taking the time. I have a few questions to understand 
your business so I can give you an accurate proposal. This should 
take about 20 minutes. Sound good?"
```

### Section 1: Business Basics (3 min)

| Question | What You're Learning |
|----------|---------------------|
| "What's your business name?" | Identity |
| "In one sentence, what do you do?" | Clarity of offering |
| "How long have you been in business?" | Stability |
| "Where are you located / who do you serve?" | Market |
| "How many people on your team?" | Scale |

### Section 2: Current State (5 min)

| Question | What You're Learning |
|----------|---------------------|
| "What's your current setup?" | Starting point |
| "What's wrong with it?" | Pain points |
| "What tools do you use?" | Tech stack |
| "How do customers find you today?" | Acquisition |
| "What's your biggest frustration?" | Hot button |

### Section 3: Goals (5 min)

| Question | What You're Learning |
|----------|---------------------|
| "What would success look like in 90 days?" | Vision |
| "What's the #1 thing you need?" | Priority |
| "How hands-on do you want to be?" | DIY vs DFY |
| "What's your monthly budget for tools?" | Financial capacity |

### Section 4: Content & Brand (3 min)

| Question | What You're Learning |
|----------|---------------------|
| "Do you have a logo?" | Brand maturity |
| "Do you have professional photos?" | Asset availability |
| "What 3 words describe your business vibe?" | Brand direction |
| "Who are 2-3 competitors you respect?" | Reference points |

### Section 5: Timeline & Budget (3 min)

| Question | What You're Learning |
|----------|---------------------|
| "When do you need this done by?" | Urgency |
| "Is there an event/deadline?" | Real deadline |
| "What's your budget for this project?" | Fit check |
| "How did you hear about me?" | Lead source |

### Closing (2 min)

```
"Great, I have everything I need. Here's what happens next:
I'll send you a summary email today, and a proposal by [DATE].
The proposal will show exactly what you get, the timeline, and pricing.
Any questions before I let you go?"
```

## 📊 Quick Qualification Score

After the call, rate 1-5:

| Factor | Score (1-5) | Notes |
|--------|-------------|-------|
| Clarity of needs | | |
| Realistic expectations | | |
| Budget fit | | |
| Timeline fit | | |
| Communication style | | |
| **TOTAL** | /25 | |

### Scoring Guide

| Total | Action |
|-------|--------|
| 20-25 | Great fit - proceed with proposal |
| 15-19 | Okay fit - proceed with clear boundaries |
| 10-14 | Risky - consider declining or adjusting scope |
| <10 | Poor fit - politely decline |

## 📧 Follow-Up Template

Send within 2 hours of call:

```
Subject: [BUSINESS NAME] - Next Steps

Hi [NAME],

Great chatting with you about [BUSINESS NAME]!

Here's what I heard:
• You need: [MAIN DELIVERABLE]
• Your goal: [THEIR SUCCESS METRIC]
• Timeline: [WHEN THEY NEED IT]

I'll send over a proposal by [DATE] with:
• Exactly what I'll deliver
• Timeline breakdown
• Investment options

Any questions before then, just reply to this email.

Talk soon,
[YOUR NAME]
```

## 🚫 Red Flags to Watch For

| Red Flag | What to Do |
|----------|------------|
| "Can you just give me a quick quote now?" | Explain discovery is needed for accurate quote |
| "My nephew can probably do it cheaper" | They don't value the work - politely decline |
| Budget way below your minimum | "I may not be the right fit, but let me refer you to..." |
| Unrealistic timeline | Explain what's actually possible |
| Scope creep in first call | Set clear boundaries in proposal |
| Poor communication | This will only get worse |
